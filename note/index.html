(300, 300)

10 Apr 2022
tags: css scary story

A well-known urban legend amongst front-end developers is the ghost div who haunts (300, 300). They say that that div was put on the front page of www.geocities.com in 1999, at x: 300px; y: 300px; It always rendered as fast as it could, positioned itself exactly where it was asked to, but because it had (accidentally?) been assigned a z-index of -999, none of the millions of people who frequented GeoCities back then ever noticed it or appreciated it.
